Interactive Ohm’s Law Circuit

Adjust the battery voltage and resistor value. The current dots speed up or slow down based on the current, and the resistor glows more strongly as power increases.
Core relationship
I = V / R
Current increases when voltage increases, and current decreases when resistance increases.
Controls
Voltage 9.0 V
Battery potential difference across the circuit.
Resistance 20.0 Ω
Higher resistance opposes the flow of current more strongly.

Teaching notes
Current dots represent charge flow visually Resistor glow scales with power Closed switch completes the loop Open switch stops current

This is a simplified classroom model. It is designed to visualize Ohm’s law clearly rather than reproduce every detail of real circuit behavior.
